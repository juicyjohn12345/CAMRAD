    NHS radiology departments across England are under sustained pressure. Demand for MRI and CT imaging has grown steadily, while consultant radiologist numbers have not kept pace. The result is a growing reporting backlog that affects patient pathways, waiting times, and clinical outcomes.

    The scale of the problem

    Across Northwest England, many trusts are reporting waits of several weeks for routine outpatient MRI reports. This delays diagnosis, increases anxiety for patients, and adds pressure to already stretched clinical teams. The problem is not new — but it is worsening.

    Where teleradiology fits in

    Teleradiology offers a flexible, scalable solution. By connecting NHS trusts with GMC-registered consultant radiologists working remotely, services like CAMRAD can deliver high-quality reports within 72 hours for routine studies — without the overhead of recruitment, onboarding, or locum agencies.

    Quality is non-negotiable

    At CAMRAD, every report is produced by a UK GMC-registered consultant radiologist, formatted using structured RCR-aligned templates, and subject to a 5% peer review programme. This is not a volume play — it is a quality-first service designed to integrate seamlessly with your existing PACS and RIS infrastructure.

    Getting started

    If your trust is facing a reporting backlog, we would welcome the opportunity to discuss how CAMRAD can help. We offer transparent, per-study pricing with no hidden fees and no minimum commitment for initial engagements.
